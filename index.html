Hola, I'm Juan David

I'm a biologist from Manizales, Caldas, Colombia, broadly interested in molecular biology, bioinformatics, evolution, phylogenetics and ecology, disease ecology and biodiversity, mammalogy, and animal welfare — with a research focus on neotropical pathogens and diseases.

My work centers on molecular biology and microbiome description tied to disease and pathogen interactions, alongside a strong interest in bioinformatics: genomics, metagenomics, and transcriptomics, from 16S sequencing to RT-qPCR and gene expression analysis.

I have worked as a Research Assistant at Mount Saint Vincent University (MSVU), Canada, and I taught molecular biology and microbiology laboratory sessions as an Instructor for the Genes and Genomes Lab. I am currently recognized as Investigador Junior by Minciencias, Colombia.

Earlier in my career I worked in field and museum-based mammalogy at Universidad de Caldas, sampling wild mammals and birds in the Colombian Orinoquia and supporting specimen preparation at the Museum of Natural History, alongside I helped during the COVID pandemic as a molecular analyst. I'm a native Spanish speaker with advanced English.

In research publications you can find me as Juan D. Carvajal-Agudelo.
